About us

About Safar Bangla

Safar Bangla is a comprehensive travel management platform designed to simplify travel planning across Bangladesh. It offers seamless booking for buses, trains, flights, and hotels, along with guided tour packages for Sundarbans and other destinations. Users can compare transport options, book accommodations, and review their experiences. The admin panel allows full control over bookings, pricing, and customer management. Safar Bangla ensures a hassle-free and efficient travel experience with a user-friendly interface and secure payment system.

Hiron Point

Hiron Point, also known as Nilkamal, is a famous wildlife sanctuary in the Sundarbans, Bangladesh. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a prime location for spotting the Royal Bengal Tiger, spotted deer, wild boars, and various bird species, including migratory birds. Surrounded by dense mangrove forests and river channels, it offers breathtaking natural beauty and is a paradise for nature lovers and photographers. The tranquil environment and rich biodiversity make Hiron Point a must-visit destination for eco-tourism. 🌿🐅

Karamjal Point

Karamjal is a popular eco-tourism spot in the Sundarbans, Bangladesh. It serves as a wildlife breeding and conservation center, especially for crocodiles and deer. Visitors can explore the mangrove forest, walk along wooden trails, and observe diverse wildlife, including monkeys, birds, and reptiles. Karamjal is an ideal place to experience the unique beauty of the Sundarbans ecosystem while learning about conservation efforts. 🌿🐊

Dublar Char

Dublar Char is a small island in the Sundarbans, famous for its fishing activities and natural beauty. It is known for the annual Rash Mela, a religious festival celebrated by Hindu devotees. The island is home to a large community of fishermen who set up seasonal fishing camps. With its pristine beaches, rich biodiversity, and a unique blend of culture and nature, Dublar Char is a fascinating destination for travelers exploring the Sundarbans. 🌊🐟

Sundarban National Park

Sundarban National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest mangrove forest in the world, located in the delta region of India and Bangladesh. It is home to the famous Royal Bengal Tiger, as well as diverse wildlife like crocodiles, deer, and various bird species. The park is known for its dense mangrove trees, tidal rivers, and unique biodiversity. It serves as a crucial ecosystem for marine and terrestrial life and plays a significant role in protecting coastal areas from storms and erosion. Sundarban National Park is a paradise for wildlife lovers and adventure seekers. 🌿🐅🌊

Kotka Beach

Kotka Beach is one of the most scenic and popular tourist spots in the Sundarbans, located along the Bay of Bengal. It is known for its golden sandy shores, serene atmosphere, and breathtaking sunset views. The beach is a prime spot for spotting wildlife, including deer, monkeys, and various bird species. Nearby, the Kotka Wildlife Sanctuary offers a chance to explore the rich biodiversity of the Sundarbans, including the elusive Royal Bengal Tiger. Kotka Beach is a perfect destination for nature lovers, photographers, and adventure seekers looking to experience the untouched beauty of the Sundarbans. 🌊🏝️🐅

Jamtola sea Beach

Jamtola Beach is a hidden gem in the Sundarbans, located near the famous Sundarbans Reserve Forest. Known for its tranquil environment, Jamtola Beach offers golden sandy shores, surrounded by lush green mangroves and serene waters. It is a less crowded spot compared to other beaches, making it an ideal place for a peaceful retreat and a closer connection with nature. The beach is also a great location for birdwatching, as it is home to many species of migratory birds. Visitors can enjoy scenic walks along the shore, and it is a fantastic spot to experience the beauty of the Sundarbans in its purest form. 🌅🐦🌊
